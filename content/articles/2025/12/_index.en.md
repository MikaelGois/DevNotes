December is the twelfth and final month of the year in the Gregorian calendar, lasting 31 days. December owes its name to the Latin word *decem* (ten), as it was the tenth month of the Roman calendar, which began in March.

December contains the summer solstice in the Southern Hemisphere, as well as the Christmas and New Year festivities, which are celebrated in many countries around the world. In the Northern Hemisphere, December is a winter month, while in the Southern Hemisphere it is a summer month.
